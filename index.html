    Modelling of Wurster Coater and Sparger Design – SUN PHARMA

    • Developed advanced CFD and DEM simulation models for the Wurster coater to address coating inefficiencies such as agglomeration, static charge buildup, and non-uniform coating distribution in pharmaceutical pellets.
    • Designed and implemented three predictive models:
      • An Excel-based agglomeration model using evaporation kinetics to define safe operational zones.
      • A MATLAB-based coating kinetics model to predict coating weight gain and batch completion time.
      • A Python-based intraparticle coating variability model using spherical harmonics to assess coating uniformity.
    • Reduced trial-and-error experiments by enabling pre-screening of operating conditions, improving lab productivity and reducing material wastage.
    • Engineered a scalable sparger system for Dichloromethane (DCM) removal from aqueous liposome solutions using inert nitrogen gas sparging, optimizing mass transfer through bubble size and sparger geometry control.
    • Derived and validated a scale-independent empirical equation for DCM stripping using dimensionless analysis (KLa ∝ (Qg/V)α) and applied it for commercial-scale sparger design.
    • Performed scale-up calculations for inline homogenizers, sparger systems, and gas-liquid mass transfer from lab-scale to commercial-scale using process engineering principles.
    • Collaborated with cross-functional R&D teams and external vendors to refine modeling approaches, validate simulation results, and ensure effective design and technology transfer.
    • Applied core chemical engineering principles to real-world pharmaceutical challenges, combining modeling, simulation, and equipment design while adhering to GMP and safety protocols.
